Cable Driving Parallel Robots were my first real research experience. My first year masters research project with Dr. Stephane Caro was intended to assist with the design of a CDPR platform being designed for the lab.

Optimal Design

As cables are only able to pull and cannot push, there are inherent limitations to CDPRs which are unlike those of classical robots.

Reconfiguration Planning

The fact that cables may only pull implies that generally cables must go in many directions in order to control the platform. This naturally means that if one is to move the platform around obstacles, care must be taken to avoid collisions between the obstables and the cables. The concept was to mount winches on the platform, and maneuver around obstacles using four cables connected to discreet anchor points on a outside frame. When a cable is changed between anchor points, the other three cables must be able to support the platform to prevent it from translating or rotating.
