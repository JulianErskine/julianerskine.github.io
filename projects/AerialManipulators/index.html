Aerial Cable-Suspended Systems

The objective of this research project was to
  1. Develop and test a method for analysing the payload capacities of a system of quadrotors manipulating a suspended payload (ACTS) under static and dynamic conditions
  2. Build a prototype ACTS using open-source drone components
  3. Program a controller for the ACTS to experimentally validate the kinematic analysis
This work was successfully completed through the end of my Master thesis and the first several month of my PhD, leading to publications of both the theoretical and experimental results. The drone system I implemented was then extended to the majority of the drone-related projects in the laboratory.

Funding

This work was funded by the Ecole Centrale de Nantes through a M.Sc research scholarship.
